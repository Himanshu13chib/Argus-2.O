## Introduction

### Purpose
This privacy policy template establishes the framework for protecting individual privacy rights while maintaining national security objectives through Project Argus border detection operations. It ensures compliance with applicable privacy laws and regulations while enabling effective border security monitoring.

### Privacy-by-Design Principles
Project Argus implements privacy-by-design principles:
- **Proactive not Reactive**: Privacy measures built into system design
- **Privacy as the Default**: Maximum privacy protection by default
- **Data Minimization**: Collect only necessary data for security purposes
- **Transparency**: Clear communication about data practices
- **Individual Rights**: Respect for individual privacy rights where legally permissible

## Data Collection and Processing

### Automated Data Collection
Project Argus automatically collects data through:

#### Camera Systems
- **Visible Light Cameras**: Facial recognition, person detection
- **Thermal Cameras**: Heat signatures, night vision detection
- **Infrared Cameras**: Low-light and night operations
- **Multi-spectral Sensors**: Enhanced detection capabilities

#### Processing Activities
- **Real-time Detection**: Person identification and tracking
- **Behavioral Analysis**: Movement pattern recognition
- **Risk Assessment**: Threat level evaluation
- **Cross-Camera Tracking**: Multi-point surveillance
- **Re-identification**: Person matching across cameras

### Data Minimization Practices

#### Default Privacy Settings
- Store only low-resolution thumbnails by default
- Retain person embeddings instead of full images
- Automatic deletion of unconfirmed incidents
- Anonymization for analytics and reporting

#### Graduated Data Collection
1. **Level 1 (Routine Monitoring)**
   - Basic detection metadata
   - Low-resolution thumbnails
   - Movement trajectories
   - 24-hour retention for unconfirmed events

2. **Level 2 (Confirmed Incidents)**
   - High-resolution imagery
   - Extended video sequences
   - Detailed metadata
   - 7-year retention period

3. **Level 3 (Legal Proceedings)**
   - Complete evidence packages
   - Full chain of custody
   - Comprehensive documentation
   - Extended retention as legally required

## Data Retention and Deletion

### Retention Schedules

#### Unconfirmed Detections
- **Retention Period**: 24 hours maximum
- **Automatic Deletion**: System-automated purging
- **Exception**: Ongoing investigations (with authorization)
- **Verification**: Daily deletion confirmation reports

#### Confirmed Security Incidents
- **Retention Period**: 7 years from incident closure
- **Review Schedule**: Annual necessity review
- **Extension Criteria**: Ongoing legal proceedings
- **Disposal Method**: Secure cryptographic deletion

#### System Logs and Metadata
- **Operational Logs**: 1 year retention
- **Audit Trails**: 10 years retention
- **Performance Metrics**: 3 years retention
- **Error Logs**: 6 months retention

### Deletion Procedures

#### Automated Deletion Process
1. **Daily Purge Cycle**
   - Identify expired unconfirmed data
   - Verify no ongoing investigations
   - Execute cryptographic deletion
   - Generate deletion confirmation logs

2. **Scheduled Reviews**
   - Monthly retention compliance audit
   - Annual necessity assessment
   - Legal hold verification
   - Disposal documentation

#### Manual Deletion Requests
1. **Authorization Required**
   - Legal department approval
   - Operational necessity review
   - Compliance verification
   - Senior management authorization

2. **Deletion Process**
   - Secure data identification
   - Chain of custody verification
   - Cryptographic deletion execution
   - Audit trail documentation

## Individual Rights

### Rights Framework
Individual rights are balanced against national security requirements and legal limitations.

#### Information Rights
**Right to Know:**
- General information about Project Argus operations
- Privacy policy and data practices
- Contact information for inquiries
- Complaint and appeal procedures

**Limitations:**
- Specific operational details (national security)
- Real-time monitoring locations
- Technical capabilities and limitations
- Ongoing investigation information

#### Access and Correction Rights
**Right to Access:**
- Confirmed incident records (with legal authorization)
- Personal data held beyond retention periods
- Correction of factual inaccuracies
- Verification of data processing compliance

**Process:**
1. Submit formal request through designated channels
2. Identity verification and authorization check
3. National security review and clearance
4. Provide available information within legal limits

#### Complaint and Appeal Rights
**Internal Complaints:**
- Privacy officer review process
- Internal investigation procedures
- Response within 30 days
- Appeal to senior management

**External Complaints:**
- Data protection authority (where applicable)
- Ombudsman or oversight body
- Judicial review (as legally available)
- International human rights mechanisms
